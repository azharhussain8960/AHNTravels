Visit Your Favorite Country With AHN Global Travels

Buenos Aires

Buenos Aires, the capital city of Argentina, is known for its European-style architecture and rich cultural life. The city offers vibrant tango shows, historical landmarks like Casa Rosada and Teatro Colón, and bustling neighborhoods such as San Telmo and La Boca.

Córdoba

Córdoba, Argentina's second-largest city, is renowned for its colonial architecture, vibrant arts scene, and lively student population. The city's highlights include the Jesuit Block, Córdoba Cathedral, and Sarmiento Park.

Mendoza

Mendoza is famous for its wine production, particularly Malbec, and stunning Andean scenery. The city offers numerous wineries and vineyards, as well as outdoor activities like hiking, rafting, and skiing.

Rosario

Rosario, located along the Paraná River, is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant arts scene. The city features the National Flag Memorial, beautiful riverfront parks, and a lively nightlife. Rosario's history as the birthplace of Che Guevara adds to its unique character.

San Carlos de Bariloche

San Carlos de Bariloche, often simply called Bariloche, is a picturesque city in the Argentine Patagonia. Known for its stunning alpine scenery, the city offers outdoor activities such as skiing, hiking, and fishing.

Salta

Salta, in northwestern Argentina, is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture and Andean heritage. The city features landmarks such as the Salta Cathedral, San Francisco Church, and the High Mountain Archaeological Museum.

Ushuaia

Ushuaia, the southernmost city in the world, is located on the Tierra del Fuego archipelago. Known as the "End of the World," the city offers dramatic landscapes, including the Beagle Channel and Tierra del Fuego National Park.

Mar del Plata

Mar del Plata is Argentina's premier beach resort city, known for its beautiful Atlantic coastline, lively nightlife, and cultural events. The city offers a range of activities, from sunbathing and surfing to exploring museums and theaters.
