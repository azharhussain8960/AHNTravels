Visit Your Favorite Country With AHN Global Travels

Cairo

Cairo, the capital city of Egypt, is known for its rich history and vibrant culture. The city is home to the iconic Giza Pyramids and the Great Sphinx, which are among the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The Egyptian Museum houses a vast collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts, including the treasures of Tutankhamun.

Alexandria

Alexandria, Egypt’s second-largest city, is known for its Mediterranean coastline and rich Greco-Roman history. The city is home to the historic Library of Alexandria and the impressive Citadel of Qaitbay, which offers stunning views of the sea. The Alexandria National Museum provides insights into the city’s past, and the Corniche, a waterfront promenade, is perfect for leisurely strolls. Montaza Palace and its gardens are a must-visit for their beauty and tranquility.

Luxor

Luxor, often referred to as the world's greatest open-air museum, is located on the east bank of the Nile River. The city is home to the magnificent temples of Karnak and Luxor, as well as the Valley of the Kings, where the tombs of Pharaohs, including Tutankhamun, are found. The Luxor Museum showcases a collection of artifacts from the ancient city of Thebes. The vibrant local markets and a sunset felucca ride on the Nile provide an unforgettable experience.

Aswan

Aswan, located in southern Egypt, is known for its beautiful scenery along the Nile River and its relaxed atmosphere. The city is home to the Philae Temple, dedicated to the goddess Isis, and the Aswan High Dam, a marvel of modern engineering. Visitors can explore the Nubian Museum, which highlights the culture and history of the Nubian people, and take a boat trip to the stunning Elephantine Island. The colorful local markets are perfect for shopping for traditional crafts and souvenirs.

Sharm El Sheikh

Sharm El Sheikh, located on the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, is a popular resort city known for its crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life. The city is famous for its world-class diving and snorkeling sites, such as Ras Mohammed National Park and the SS Thistlegorm wreck. Naama Bay offers a lively nightlife scene with numerous restaurants, cafes, and nightclubs. The nearby desert provides opportunities for quad biking, camel rides, and stargazing.

Hurghada

Hurghada, a resort town along Egypt's Red Sea coast, is renowned for its stunning coral reefs and turquoise waters. The city is a hub for water sports, including diving, snorkeling, and windsurfing. The Marina Boulevard offers a range of dining and shopping options, while the Hurghada Grand Aquarium showcases the marine life of the Red Sea. The nearby Giftun Islands are a popular destination for day trips, offering beautiful beaches and excellent snorkeling opportunities.

Giza

Giza, located on the outskirts of Cairo, is world-famous for the Giza Plateau, which houses the Great Pyramids and the Sphinx. These ancient wonders attract millions of visitors each year. The Solar Boat Museum displays a reconstructed ancient Egyptian ship. The city also offers a range of cultural experiences, including camel rides and traditional Egyptian cuisine at local restaurants. Giza's close proximity to Cairo makes it easy to explore both areas in one trip.

Dahab

Dahab, a small town on the southeast coast of the Sinai Peninsula, is known for its laid-back atmosphere and excellent diving spots. The Blue Hole, one of the world’s most famous dive sites, is located here. Dahab’s coastline is perfect for windsurfing, snorkeling, and other water sports. The town's Bedouin culture can be experienced through desert excursions and local dining. The relaxed vibe and beautiful natural scenery make Dahab a favorite among backpackers and adventurers.

Siwa Oasis

Siwa Oasis, located in the Western Desert of Egypt, is known for its unique culture and stunning natural beauty. The oasis is home to the ancient Oracle of Amun temple and the ruins of Shali Fortress. The salt lakes and hot springs provide a refreshing experience in the desert landscape. Siwa's local culture, influenced by the Berber people, offers a unique insight into traditional desert life. The oasis is a serene getaway, perfect for those looking to escape the bustling cities.
