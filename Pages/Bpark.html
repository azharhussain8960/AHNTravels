Bannerghatta National Park

Bannerghatta National Park, Bangalore

Bannerghatta National Park, located near Bangalore in Karnataka, India, is a sprawling wildlife sanctuary that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and wildlife experiences. Established in 1970 and declared a national park in 1974, Bannerghatta covers an area of over 104.27 square kilometers, encompassing a diverse range of flora and fauna.

The park is home to a variety of animals, including elephants, leopards, lions, tigers, deer, and numerous species of birds and reptiles. One of the main attractions is the Bannerghatta Biological Park, which includes a zoo, a safari park, a butterfly park, and a rescue center for animals. The safari offers visitors the chance to see lions, tigers, and other animals in their natural habitat from the safety of a vehicle.

Bannerghatta National Park also boasts the country's first butterfly park, which features a conservatory, an audiovisual room, and a museum. This park is home to hundreds of butterfly species and provides a wonderful opportunity to observe these colorful insects up close.

For nature enthusiasts, the park offers several trekking and hiking trails that meander through its lush forests and hills. These trails provide breathtaking views and the chance to spot various wildlife species in their natural surroundings. Additionally, the park's rich biodiversity makes it a popular destination for birdwatching.

Bannerghatta National Park is an excellent destination for families, wildlife lovers, and adventure seekers. The park's varied attractions and activities ensure that there is something for everyone, whether it's exploring the zoo, going on a safari, trekking through the forests, or simply enjoying a picnic in the serene environment.
