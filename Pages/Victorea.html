Angel of Victory, Victoria Memorial

Angel of Victory, Victoria Memorial

The Angel of Victory is an iconic sculpture that graces the top of the Victoria Memorial, one of Kolkata's most famous landmarks. This grand marble building, dedicated to Queen Victoria, is not only a museum but also a splendid architectural marvel that blends British and Mughal styles.

The Angel of Victory is a 16-foot tall, bronze statue designed by Sir Thomas Brock. It represents a winged angel holding a trumpet, symbolizing the spirit of victory. The statue is mounted on a revolving pedestal, and it gracefully rotates with the wind, adding a dynamic element to the otherwise static structure of the memorial.

The Victoria Memorial, built between 1906 and 1921, is surrounded by lush gardens and beautiful fountains, making it a serene spot in the bustling city of Kolkata. The memorial itself is home to a vast collection of artifacts, paintings, and memorabilia from the British era, offering visitors a glimpse into India's colonial history.

The Angel of Victory stands as a testament to the grandeur and historical significance of the Victoria Memorial. Its graceful presence and the architectural beauty of the memorial attract millions of visitors every year, making it a must-visit landmark in Kolkata.

Whether you're an architecture enthusiast, a history buff, or simply looking to enjoy a peaceful day in a picturesque setting, the Victoria Memorial and the Angel of Victory provide a captivating experience that reflects the rich cultural heritage of India.
