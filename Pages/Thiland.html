Visit Your Favorite Country With AHN Global Travels

Bangkok

Bangkok, the capital city of Thailand, is known for its vibrant street life, cultural landmarks, and bustling markets. Popular attractions include the Grand Palace, Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn), Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha), and the bustling Khao San Road. The city is also famous for its shopping malls, night markets, and delicious street food.

Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai, located in northern Thailand, is known for its beautiful temples, lush landscapes, and vibrant cultural scene. Key attractions include Wat Phra That Doi Suthep, the Old City with its ancient walls and moats, and the bustling Night Bazaar. Chiang Mai is also famous for its festivals, including the Yi Peng Lantern Festival and Songkran (Thai New Year).

Phuket

Phuket is Thailand's largest island and a popular beach destination known for its stunning coastline, vibrant nightlife, and water activities. Major attractions include Patong Beach, Big Buddha, and Phi Phi Islands. The island also offers luxury resorts, lively markets, and adventure activities such as diving, snorkeling, and island hopping.

Pattaya

Pattaya is a resort city on Thailand's eastern Gulf coast known for its beaches, nightlife, and water sports. Popular attractions include the Pattaya Beach, Walking Street, Sanctuary of Truth, and Nong Nooch Tropical Garden. The city offers a range of entertainment options, from cabaret shows to water parks and adventure activities.

Ayutthaya

Ayutthaya, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is known for its historical ruins and ancient temples. Key attractions include Wat Mahathat, Wat Phra Si Sanphet, and Ayutthaya Historical Park. The city offers a glimpse into Thailand's rich history and is a popular destination for cultural and historical exploration.

Krabi

Krabi is known for its stunning limestone cliffs, clear waters, and beautiful beaches. Major attractions include Railay Beach, Ao Nang, and the Phi Phi Islands. Krabi also offers opportunities for rock climbing, kayaking, and exploring natural parks such as the Thung Teao Forest Natural Park.

Chiang Rai

Chiang Rai is known for its remarkable temples and natural beauty. Key attractions include the White Temple (Wat Rong Khun), the Blue Temple (Wat Rong Suea Ten), and the Black House (Baan Dam Museum). The city is also a gateway to exploring the Golden Triangle region, where the borders of Thailand, Laos, and Myanmar meet.

Hua Hin

Hua Hin is a seaside resort town known for its royal heritage and beautiful beaches. Major attractions include the Hua Hin Beach, Cicada Market, and the historic Railway Station. The town also offers a range of outdoor activities, such as golfing, kiteboarding, and visiting the nearby Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park.

Sukhothai

Sukhothai is known for its historical significance and ancient ruins. The Sukhothai Historical Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features well-preserved ruins of palaces, temples, and monuments from the Sukhothai Kingdom. The park's tranquil atmosphere and cultural heritage make it a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Kanchanaburi

Kanchanaburi is known for its historical sites related to World War II and its natural beauty. The Bridge over the River Kwai, the Death Railway, and the JEATH War Museum are significant historical attractions. The city also offers stunning natural scenery, including Erawan National Park with its beautiful waterfalls and the tranquil Sai Yok National Park.
