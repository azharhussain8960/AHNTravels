Visit Your Favorite Country With AHN Global Travels

Addis Ababa

Addis Ababa, the capital city of Ethiopia, is known for its rich history and cultural diversity. The city is home to several significant landmarks, including the National Museum, where the famous fossil Lucy is housed, and the Holy Trinity Cathedral.

Lalibela

Lalibela, a town in northern Ethiopia, is renowned for its rock-hewn churches, which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The city offers attractions such as the Church of Saint George, one of the eleven monolithic churches, and the Lalibela Cross.

Gondar

Gondar, known as the "Camelot of Africa," is famous for its medieval castles and churches. The city offers attractions such as the Fasil Ghebbi, a fortress complex with several castles, and the Church of Debre Berhan Selassie, known for its beautiful murals.

Axum

Axum, an ancient city in northern Ethiopia, is known for its archaeological sites and historical significance. The city offers attractions such as the Obelisks of Axum, ancient stelae dating back to the 4th century, and the Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion, believed to house the Ark of the Covenant.

Bahir Dar

Bahir Dar, located on the shores of Lake Tana, is known for its scenic beauty and historical monasteries. The city offers attractions such as the Blue Nile Falls, one of Africa's most impressive waterfalls, and the ancient monasteries on the Lake Tana islands.

Harar

Harar, a city in eastern Ethiopia, is known for its historical walls and vibrant culture. The city offers attractions such as the Harar Jugol, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with 82 mosques and over 100 shrines, and the Hyena Man spectacle, where visitors can feed wild hyenas.

Dire Dawa

Dire Dawa, one of Ethiopia's largest cities, is known for its historical railway and diverse architecture. The city offers attractions such as the Dire Dawa Railway Station, part of the historic Ethio-Djibouti Railway, and the Kefira Market, one of the largest markets in Ethiopia.

Mekelle

Mekelle, the capital of the Tigray region, is known for its historical significance and growing economy. The city offers attractions such as the Tigray People's Liberation Front (TPLF) Monument, the Emperor Yohannes IV Palace Museum, and the nearby rock-hewn churches of Tigray.

Awassa

Awassa, also known as Hawassa, is a beautiful city located on the shores of Lake Awassa in southern Ethiopia. The city offers attractions such as the Awassa Lake, popular for birdwatching and boat trips, and the Awassa Fish Market.

Jimma

Jimma, a city in southwestern Ethiopia, is known for its coffee plantations and historical significance. The city offers attractions such as the Jimma Museum, showcasing artifacts from the region's history, and the Aba Jifar Palace, the former residence of King Aba Jifar II.
