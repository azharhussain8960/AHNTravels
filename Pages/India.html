Visit Your Favorite Country With AHN Global Travels

New Delhi

The capital city of India, New Delhi is known for its historic landmarks, such as the Red Fort, India Gate, Qutub Minar, and Humayun's Tomb. It is a bustling metropolis with a mix of modern amenities and ancient architecture. The city is also famous for its vibrant markets, delicious street food, and cultural festivals.

Mumbai

Mumbai, formerly known as Bombay, is the financial and entertainment capital of India. It is home to Bollywood, the worldâ€™s largest film industry. Key attractions include the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, Elephanta Caves, and the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. The city is also known for its lively street markets and bustling nightlife.

Agra

Agra is renowned worldwide for the iconic Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Other attractions include the Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri. The city is a major destination on the Golden Triangle tourist circuit, along with Delhi and Jaipur.

Jaipur

Known as the "Pink City" for its distinct pink-colored buildings, Jaipur is famous for its royal palaces, forts, and vibrant bazaars. Major attractions include the Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Jantar Mantar. Jaipur is also a part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit.

Varanasi

Varanasi, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, is a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. The city is famous for its ghats along the Ganges River, where pilgrims perform rituals and ceremonies. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sarnath, and the evening Ganga Aarti are major attractions.

Bangalore (Bengaluru)

Known as the "Silicon Valley of India," Bangalore is the major hub for the IT industry. The city is also known for its pleasant climate, green spaces, and vibrant nightlife. Key attractions include Lalbagh Botanical Garden, Bangalore Palace, and the ISKCON Temple.

Kolkata

Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, is the cultural capital of India. It is known for its colonial architecture, literary heritage, and festivals like Durga Puja. Major attractions include the Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, Indian Museum, and Dakshineswar Kali Temple.

Chennai

Chennai, the capital of Tamil Nadu, is known for its beaches, temples, and classical music and dance. Marina Beach, Kapaleeshwarar Temple, and Fort St. George are major attractions. The city is also a gateway to exploring South India's rich cultural heritage.

Hyderabad

Known as the "City of Pearls," Hyderabad is famous for its historic landmarks, such as the Charminar, Golconda Fort, and the Qutb Shahi Tombs. The city also offers the Ramoji Film City, one of the largest film studio complexes in the world, and is known for its delicious biryani.

Udaipur

Often referred to as the "City of Lakes," Udaipur is famous for its stunning lakes, palaces, and heritage hotels. Major attractions include the City Palace, Lake Pichola, Jag Mandir, and the Saheliyon-ki-Bari. The cityâ€™s romantic ambiance makes it a popular wedding and honeymoon destination.

Goa

Goa is renowned for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. Popular beaches include Baga, Anjuna, and Palolem. Old Goaâ€™s colonial architecture, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus and Se Cathedral, reflects its rich history.

Amritsar

Amritsar is home to the Golden Temple, the holiest shrine of Sikhism. The city also offers the Jallianwala Bagh memorial and the Wagah Border ceremony. The bustling markets and delicious Punjabi cuisine are additional attractions.

Mysore

Known for its royal heritage, Mysore features the stunning Mysore Palace, Chamundi Hill, and the bustling Devaraja Market. The city is also famous for its Dasara festival, yoga centers, and sandalwood products.

Kerala (Cochin/Kochi)

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a major port city with a rich colonial history. Key attractions include Fort Kochi, Chinese fishing nets, Mattancherry Palace, and the backwaters. Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," offers lush landscapes, houseboat cruises, and Ayurvedic wellness.

Rishikesh

Rishikesh, known as the "Yoga Capital of the World," is a major center for spirituality and adventure tourism. The city offers yoga and meditation retreats, river rafting on the Ganges, and the iconic Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula bridges.
