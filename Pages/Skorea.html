Visit Your Favorite Country With AHN Global Travels

Seoul

Seoul, the capital city of South Korea, is known for its vibrant culture, historic palaces, and modern skyscrapers. Popular attractions include Gyeongbokgung Palace, N Seoul Tower, Bukchon Hanok Village, and Myeongdong shopping district. The city offers a blend of traditional Korean culture and contemporary amenities.

Busan

Busan is a coastal city known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant seafood markets, and cultural festivals. Key attractions include Haeundae Beach, Gamcheon Culture Village, Jagalchi Fish Market, and Beomeosa Temple. The city offers a mix of urban life and natural beauty, with scenic mountains and seaside views.

Incheon

Incheon is known for its modern architecture, historic sites, and proximity to Seoul. Major attractions include Incheon Bridge, Songdo Central Park, Wolmido Island, and the Incheon Chinatown. The city offers a blend of new urban developments and cultural heritage.

Jeju City

Jeju City is the capital of Jeju Island, known for its volcanic landscapes, beautiful beaches, and unique culture. Popular attractions include Hallasan Mountain, Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak), Jeju Folk Village, and Manjanggul Cave. The island offers a serene environment with outdoor activities and scenic natural beauty.

Gyeongju

Gyeongju is known as the "museum without walls" due to its rich history and numerous cultural sites. Key attractions include Bulguksa Temple, Seokguram Grotto, Anapji Pond, and the Gyeongju National Museum. The city offers a glimpse into Korea's ancient past with its well-preserved relics and historic sites.

Daegu

Daegu is known for its traditional markets, cultural festivals, and modern shopping districts. Major attractions include Donghwasa Temple, Seomun Market, E-World, and Apsan Park. The city offers a mix of historical sites, natural beauty, and urban entertainment.

Daejeon

Daejeon is known for its science and technology centers, hot springs, and cultural landmarks. Popular attractions include Expo Park, Yuseong Hot Springs, Daejeon O-World, and the Hanbat Arboretum. The city offers a blend of educational attractions and recreational activities.

Suwon

Suwon is known for its historical sites, particularly the UNESCO-listed Hwaseong Fortress. Key attractions include Hwaseong Haenggung Palace, Suwon World Cup Stadium, and the Korean Folk Village. The city offers a mix of cultural heritage and modern amenities.

Gwangju

Gwangju is known for its vibrant arts scene, historic sites, and cultural festivals. Major attractions include the Gwangju Biennale, May 18th National Cemetery, Gwangju Folk Museum, and Mudeungsan National Park. The city offers a blend of contemporary art, history, and natural beauty.

Jeonju

Jeonju is famous for its well-preserved Hanok Village, traditional Korean food, and cultural heritage. Popular attractions include Jeonju Hanok Village, Jeondong Catholic Church, Gyeonggijeon Shrine, and the Jeonju National Museum. The city offers a rich cultural experience with its traditional architecture and culinary delights.
